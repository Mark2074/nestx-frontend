Introduction

Live rooms and events are core tools on NestX. They enable real-time interaction, audience building, and participation.

Every live session and event must follow platform rules, applicable laws, and the core principles of authenticity and consent.

4.1 Live Rooms

Live Rooms are real-time sessions where a user can broadcast and interact with others.

Live Rooms may be public or access-limited, and may be linked to specific events. Depending on platform rules and configuration, a live session can be adult or non-adult.

  • All interactions must remain respectful and compliant with platform rules.
  • Illegal, coercive, or manipulative behavior is not allowed.
  • The host is responsible for the content shown and the tone of the session.
Reminder
Live sessions are not “outside the rules”. Moderation and enforcement can apply during active sessions.

4.2 Events

An Event is a scheduled session with a more defined structure than a standard live room.

Events may include scheduling, participation rules, a defined duration, and where applicable, ticketed access.

  • Events must respect content rules, behavior rules, and moderation.
  • An event does not automatically grant Creator privileges.
  • An event does not guarantee any economic outcome or results.

4.3 Adult vs Non-Adult Content

NestX may host adult content within clear boundaries. The core requirements are:

  • All participants must be 18+.
  • Consent must be real and never coerced.
  • No illegal, exploitative, or unsafe content is allowed.

Non-adult events and live rooms must remain coherent with their category. Misclassification or attempts to bypass rules may result in platform action.

4.4 What Is Not Allowed

Live rooms and events must not be used for:

  • Escorting or prostitution-related services.
  • Unauthorized marketplaces for goods or services.
  • Dating activity disguised as “content”.
  • Coordination of illegal activity.
  • Any form of exploitation, coercion, or manipulation.
Clarity
NestX is not a dating service and not a marketplace. Live rooms and events exist for content and participation, within platform rules.

4.5 Copyright and Third-Party Content

Live sessions and events must also respect copyright and other third-party rights.

Users and creators should avoid broadcasting music, television channels, radio streams, sports events, movies, clips, or other third-party material during live sessions or events unless they have the legal right or license to do so.

This also includes background audio or media captured during a live session, for example music playing in the room, content from streaming services, or a television left on in the background.

NestX does not provide music, broadcast, or public performance licenses. Responsibility for third-party content used during live sessions or events remains with the user who streams it.

Tip
To avoid unnecessary issues, keep background audio minimal and avoid using copyrighted music or media during live sessions unless you clearly have the rights to use it.
